# Tally Baseball

A **spoiler-safe companion** for scoring baseball games by hand.

If you keep score in a paper scorebook, this app rides along on your phone as a
second screen. It looks up the game you're at (or watching on a delay) and shows
you everything you need to fill in your book — lineups, uniform numbers,
umpires, the bullpen and bench — **without ever spoiling the score.**

Every number that could give away what's happening stays hidden behind a cover
until you decide you're ready to see it. Tap to reveal, one inning at a time.

---

## Why it exists

Scoring by hand is the fun part — but the moment you glance at your phone to
check a batter's number or which reliever is warming up, you risk seeing the
score and ruining the surprise. Regular baseball apps are built to tell you the
score as fast as possible. This one is built to *not*.

It's a lookup tool, not a scoring tool. You still do all the scoring yourself,
on paper. This just answers the questions that come up while you do — and, when
the game's over, it turns into a full baseball reference you can wander through.

---

## Following a game

**Pick your game.** Open the app and you'll see the day's games. Flip between
the majors and the four full-season minor-league levels (AAA, AA, A+, A) with the
toggle up top, and step forward or back a day to plan ahead or look something up.
(The Brewers are pinned to the top.) Each game card tells you the start time and
whether it's live or final — but never the score, even for a game that ended days
ago. Little green checkmarks show you at a glance whether the lineups, umpires,
starters, and uniforms have been posted yet.

**Check the teams.** Before first pitch, page through each team's details:
the batting order with uniform numbers and positions, the starting pitcher (with
their season line and which hand they throw with), the manager, the umpires, and
the ballpark, weather, first-pitch time, and tonight's uniforms. There's even a
sketch of the other team's defensive alignment, drawn on a diamond. Before the
lineup is posted, it shows you the whole active roster — batters, bullpen, and
starters — so you're never staring at a blank page.

**Follow along inning by inning.** Once the game starts, move through it one
half-inning at a time. Each one shows a cover that says *"Tap to reveal."*
Until you tap it, there is no score on your screen — nothing to catch out of the
corner of your eye. When you're caught up in your book and ready, tap to uncover
that half-inning all at once:

- **Runs, hits, errors, and runners left on base**
- **Pitch count** for the inning and a running total
- **Whiffs** (swinging strikes) and **first-pitch strikes**
- **A full play-by-play** — every plate appearance, the pitch sequence, how the
  out was made, and where the runners ended up
- **Statcast bests** for the half — the fastest pitch, hardest-hit ball, and
  longest fly, wherever the ballpark tracks them

A running line score builds across the top as you go, and it doubles as a
navigator — tap any inning to jump there. A pitchers table keeps a running tally
for each staff, and a "so far" win-probability chart traces the game's swings up
to exactly where you've revealed.

**Extra innings never leak.** The app only shows the nine regulation innings up
front. Extras unlock one at a time, and only after you've revealed the inning
before — so nothing on screen hints that a game went long before you get there
yourself.

**The bullpen and bench, always handy.** A collapsible reference lists the
relievers still available (and which hand they throw) and the bench players who
haven't entered yet, plus each team's lineup and defense as they stand entering
the half you're on. It never reveals anything about the score.

**The full box score, when you want it.** One button opens a complete,
MLB-style box score — sealed until you tap. Inside: the line score, pitchers of
record, the three stars, both teams' batting and pitching lines with all the
substitutions, the defensive alignment, and the full game-info footer. There's
even a sealed "game buzz" card with the night's best posts about the game, in
case you want to jot a note in the margin.

## Exploring the rest of baseball

The app grew a whole reference section you can browse any time — all of it built
to the same spoiler rules.

**Player pages.** Tap any name — in a lineup, a box score, a leaderboard — to open
that player's page: headshot, bio, handedness, draft info, and their MLB debut.
Below that is the good stuff: this season's stat line, a pitcher's pitch mix, the
game log, splits against lefties and righties, and a complete career register
that blends their major- and minor-league seasons. A "Path to the Majors" strip
traces every level they climbed, a team-history timeline shows every club they've
worn, a transactions timeline lists every trade and move, and "firsts" links you
to the game of their first hit or first win. Prospects, All-Stars, players on
rehab, and players on the Injured List are all flagged. (Open a player's page in
the middle of a live game and their stats freeze at "before today," so tonight's
line can't sneak through.)

**Team pages.** Tap a logo to open a club's hub: its record and division rank, a
month-at-a-glance schedule, the division standings, where the team ranks across
the league in every hitting and pitching category, the leaders on the roster, the
full roster (with each player's WAR), and who's on the Injured List. Minor-league
clubs also show their parent organization, their affiliation history, and the
whole farm system.

**Standings.** Full standings for both leagues — but they open *through
yesterday*, so today's games can't spoil you. When you're ready, one tap reveals
today's live standings, and you can scrub back to any earlier date.

**League leaders.** Browse the stat leaderboards for the majors, either league,
or any minor-league level — or drill into a single organization's whole system.

**Top prospects.** The current Top 100, each row linking straight to that
player's page and current club.

**Rehab assignments.** A league-wide list of every big leaguer currently on a
minor-league rehab stint — who they are, which affiliate they're with, and since
when. Handy when a familiar name shows up in a minor-league box.

**Search and past matchups.** Search any player or team by name from the footer.
"Find a past matchup" lets you pick two clubs and a season and pulls up every game
they played — and each one opens its box score, sealed until you tap.

---

## The one promise

**A hidden number is never really on your screen until you reveal it.** It isn't
faded out, or covered by a sticker you might peek under, or sitting in the page
waiting to flash. It genuinely isn't there until you ask for it — and once an
inning re-seals, it's gone again. Even the browser tab title and the game cards
are kept score-free. The whole app is designed around not spoiling you, even by
accident.

## Installing it on your phone

It runs in your web browser, but you can add it to your home screen so it opens
full-screen like a normal app:

1. Open the app's web address in **Safari** on your iPhone.
2. Tap the **Share** button, then **Add to Home Screen.**
3. Launch it from the new icon — no app store, no sign-in.

It updates itself; just reopen it to get the latest version.

---

## A note on the minor leagues

Minor-league games don't always publish as much information as the majors —
sometimes a lineup, the weather, or a team logo just isn't posted yet. When that
happens the app simply shows a dash or "not posted yet" rather than guessing.
Check back closer to game time and the details usually fill in.

---

## Bonus: logo sketch sheets

Like to draw the team logos in your scorebook? The **Logos** button prints a
clean grayscale sheet of every team playing at a given level — a handy tracing and
sketching reference to set beside your book. Tap any logo to blow it up for
tracing. (No scores on it, of course.)
