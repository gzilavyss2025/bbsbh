Team Transactions — card wireframe, v7

Each day now headers as a full dateline — "Sunday, July 12" — instead of a month row plus an abbreviated "Jul 12" underneath. The type pill (Trade, Roster shuffle, …) and the IN / OUT headshot rail share one top edge, and the cutline sentence wraps around the headshots the way a magazine caption wraps a floated photo. Each headshot carries a soft wash of the club's brand color (Brewers gold here, the same teamTintColor() every other headshot in the app already uses) under the uniform halftone dot screen. Content is pulled from the Brewers' real June/July 2026 transaction log. Below: the same card at mobile, iPad, and desktop widths.

Rail floats, text wraps

The IN/OUT rail is a real CSS float now, not a grid column — the type pill sits flush with its top edge and the cutline sentence wraps around it, closing back to full width once the sentence runs past the photos, the way a magazine wraps body text around a floated photo.

Still no overlap

Each headshot keeps its own plain, non-overlapping frame in the rail — just side by side and narrower than before, not stacked.

Halftone instead of grayscale-vs-color

Every photo still gets the same dot-screen treatment — that's what reads as "newspaper," rather than color-coding one side. In/Out is carried by the small kicker banner over each headshot instead.

Double-rule section flag

The heavy rule under "Transactions" borrows a section-masthead cue (like a sports-page flag) instead of the plain caption-style .section__title used elsewhere — worth checking this doesn't fight the rest of the team page's header rhythm.

One-sided stories still work

A plain signing (bottom row) just leaves one slot in the rail rather than forcing a pair — most transactions won't have a clean corollary.

Multi-move days are the norm, not the edge case

Pulling the real feed for the Brewers' past three weeks turned up 6 transactions on Jul 12 and 8 on Jul 7 — so the date now headers a .txday group and each transaction underneath is its own compact story, rather than repeating the date per story.

Not every move is a clean 2-player pair

Jul 7's roster shuffle is 3 players (2 up, 1 down) in one transaction — the rail just grows another slot. Jul 12 also has a same-player double-move (activated off the IL, then optioned right back down) and a pure IL-to-IL transfer with no active-roster photo at all — that one gets no rail, just the sentence.

Real feeds duplicate records

The raw statsapi transactions endpoint logged the same Logan Henderson rehab assignment 3 times and the same trade twice (once per team). The real generator will need to de-dupe by description+date before grouping into stories — not a UI concern, but worth flagging now so the data pipeline accounts for it.

Desktop doesn't stretch the card

Same single-column structure at every width — this is a second-screen companion, not a dashboard. iPad just gives the cutline/rail more room to breathe (600px column); desktop caps the card at a comfortable ~460px reading width rather than stretching it to fill a widescreen, shown here on a dashed canvas so the unused space reads as deliberate, not missing.
